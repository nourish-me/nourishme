How it works.

  1. 1

    Three-minute onboarding

    Phase, trimester, height, weight, activity, number of children, estimated milk volume. NourishMe derives your energy and macro needs from those.

  2. 2

    Log meals by text, photo or barcode

    "Cereal with yogurt, one bowl." Done. Photos get identified automatically; packaged products pull their brand-accurate values from the Open Food Facts database. A meal can bundle several items — scan the skyr, then type the apple.

  3. 3

    The app remembers your favourites

    Start typing and NourishMe suggests products from your history. One tap and the entry lands with the exact values from last time — no new estimate needed.

  4. 4

    Get a coach hint right away

    After every meal, one specific suggestion for what to eat next, based on today's progress and your daily target.

  5. 5

    Food-safety built in

    Caffeine, alcohol wait times, mercury fish, listeria. All from official sources, no searching required.

  6. 6

    Trends at a glance

    A dedicated tab shows a weekly chart, sweet-spot streak and weekly averages for calories and macros. You can see at a glance which days you were on target.

  7. 7

    Reminders, only if you want them

    Five configurable slots for breakfast, mid-morning, lunch, afternoon and dinner. Off by default, on with one tap. Scheduled locally on your device, no cloud.

Why NourishMe exists

14 weeks ago I had twins. Leo and Carl.

From week one I kept asking myself: am I actually eating enough, for all three of us? Every nutrient goes to the babies first. I needed to stay on top of my own intake for the whole thing to work.

And there was just nothing out there that could tell me. Nutrition apps are built for weight loss. Pregnancy apps stopped the day you gave birth. Breastfeeding apps slapped on a flat +500 kcal, one baby or twins, nursing or pumping, didn't matter.

So that's how NourishMe happened.

It tells you after every meal what you and your baby still need, based on your actual data, not some generic number. All recommendations trace back to real sources: DGE, BfR, EFSA, LactMed.

Vanessa, mom to Leo and Carl

Frequently asked

Questions that keep coming up.

Where is my data stored?

Meals, profile, weight and settings live only on your iPhone, in a local on-device database. No cloud account, no sync, no server holds a copy. Delete the app and the data is gone.

Does the AI see my meals?

When you log a meal the description briefly travels to Anthropic's Claude for analysis; the reply comes back and is stored locally. Anthropic does not use API data for training (per their policy), and there's no account linking it to you. Anonymous usage stats (which methods, how often, whether coach replies arrive) go to PostHog on EU servers — without contents, weights or names. You can switch analytics off in Settings any time.

How accurate are the calorie and macro estimates?

For packaged products (barcode scan) very accurate: values come straight from the manufacturer via Open Food Facts. For text and photo entries they're estimates — solid for trends, not for medical precision. You can adjust amounts at any time and everything scales with it.

Does the app work if I'm not (or no longer) breastfeeding?

Yes. NourishMe calculates based on your phase. In your profile you can switch between pregnancy, breastfeeding and "neither" at any time, and the calorie and nutrient calculations adapt. For general adult nutrition outside these phases, more specialised apps exist.

What if I'm vegetarian, vegan or have allergies?

You can set your dietary style (omnivore, vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian) and allergies in your profile. The coach respects them absolutely: vegan suggestions contain no animal products, nut allergy means no nuts.

How much does NourishMe cost?

Free in the closed beta. For the public launch a monthly subscription with a 7-day trial is planned. Exact prices will be shared in time, and the principle stays: no ads, no data sharing.

Is NourishMe a medical product?

No. NourishMe is a personal wellness tool, not a medical device, and not a substitute for medical or midwife advice. For medical questions, high-risk pregnancy or existing conditions, talk to your doctor or midwife.
